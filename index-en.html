PTSD FAQ

What is PTSD?
Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) is a mental disorder that can develop after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. This can be military conflict, an accident, violence, a natural disaster, or other life-threatening situations.
What are the main symptoms of PTSD?
Main symptoms include: intrusive memories of the traumatic event, nightmares, avoidance of trauma reminders, negative changes in thoughts and mood, heightened vigilance and threat response.
Can PTSD be treated?
Yes, PTSD is treatable. Effective methods include psychotherapy (cognitive-behavioral therapy, EMDR), medication, and self-help techniques.
How can Neurica help with PTSD?
Neurica provides 24/7 support, helps track symptoms, offers calming techniques. It complements professional help but does not replace a psychotherapist.
When should I seek immediate help?
Seek help immediately if you have suicidal thoughts, plans to harm yourself or others, severe depression, or panic attacks that significantly worsen your daily life.
Are conversations with Neurica confidential?
Yes, all conversations are completely confidential and protected with modern encryption. We do not share personal information with third parties without your consent.
