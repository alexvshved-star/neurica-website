PTSD FAQ

What is PTSD?
Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) is a mental disorder that can develop after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. This can be military conflict, an accident, violence, a natural disaster, or other life-threatening situations.
What are the main symptoms of PTSD?
Main symptoms include: intrusive memories of the traumatic event, nightmares, avoidance of trauma reminders, negative changes in thoughts and mood, heightened vigilance and threat response. Symptoms may appear immediately after the event or months/years later.
Can PTSD be treated?
Yes, PTSD is treatable. Effective methods include psychotherapy (particularly cognitive-behavioral therapy and EMDR), medication, and various self-help techniques. It's important to seek professional help from a specialist.
How can Neurica help with PTSD?
Neurica provides 24/7 support, helps track symptoms, offers calming and self-regulation techniques, creates a safe space for expressing emotions. It's important to understand that Neurica is a complement to professional help, not a replacement for a psychotherapist.
When should I seek immediate help?
Seek immediate help from a specialist or crisis hotline if you have suicidal thoughts, plans to harm yourself or others, severe depression, panic attacks that interfere with daily life, or if symptoms are significantly worsening.
Are conversations with Neurica confidential?
Yes, all conversations with Neurica are completely confidential and protected. We use modern encryption methods and do not share personal information with third parties without your consent.
